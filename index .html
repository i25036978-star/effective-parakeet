SETTINGS

Beijing began as Ji City in the Western Zhou Dynasty, over 3,000 years ago. It became a capital for more than 800 years, first as Nanjing in the Liao, then Zhongdu in the Jin, Dadu in the Yuan, and later the capital of the Ming and Qing. Today, Beijing is China’s political, cultural, and technological center. It is backed by the Western Hills and crossed by the Yongding River – a city where ancient history meets modern innovation.

Beijing as a Cultural Heartland

As the most important carrier of Chinese culture, Beijing is often called “the finest place under heaven.” It has eight World Heritage sites, including the Forbidden City, the Great Wall, and the Central Axis. The city’s unique character lives in its hutongs and courtyards, the sounds of Peking opera and crosstalk, and the flavors of roast duck and hotpot.
